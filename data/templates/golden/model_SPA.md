## ARTICLE 4 — PERFORMANCE BOND

4.1 Within **[5] banking days** of execution of this SPA, Buyer shall wire a **Performance Bond of 2% (two percent)** of the total contract value (USD [Amount]) via Telegraphic Transfer (T/T) to Seller's designated account.

4.2 The Performance Bond shall be:
- Fully refundable upon successful completion of all shipments
- Forfeited by Seller as liquidated damages in case of Buyer's material breach
- Not forfeited if the breach is caused by Seller's non-performance

4.3 Seller's Performance Bond (counter-guarantee): In the event of Seller's failure to deliver, Seller shall refund the Performance Bond plus a penalty of **0.5% (zero point five percent) of the undelivered shipment value per day of delay**, up to a maximum of **5% (five percent)** of total contract value.

## ARTICLE 6 — INSURANCE

6.1 Seller shall procure marine insurance coverage for the cargo at a minimum of **110% (one hundred ten percent)** of the Commercial Invoice value.

6.2 Insurance shall be:
- **Type:** Institute Cargo Clauses A (All Risks)
- **Beneficiary:** Buyer
- **Coverage Period:** From port of loading to port of destination

6.3 Seller shall provide the Insurance Certificate as part of the DLC document set, per UCP 600 Art. 28.

## ARTICLE 9 — FORCE MAJEURE

9.1 Neither Party shall be liable for failure or delay in performance of **its** obligations under this Agreement where such failure results from causes beyond **its** reasonable control, including but not limited to: acts of God, war, terrorism, government regulations, port strikes, or natural disasters ("Force Majeure Event").

9.2 The affected Party shall notify the other Party within **5 (five) business days** of the occurrence of a Force Majeure Event, providing documentary evidence.

9.3 If a Force Majeure Event persists for more than **60 (sixty) days**, either Party may terminate this Agreement by written notice, with no further liability.
